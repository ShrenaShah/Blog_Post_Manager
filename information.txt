path.resolve() is a method provided by Node.js's built-in path module. 
It is used to resolve a sequence of paths or path segments into an absolute path.
    An absolute path is the complete location of a file or folder from the root directory of the system or project.
    In general, resolve means to figure out, find, or convert something unclear into something clear or final.
      In the context of path.resolve():
      It resolves (converts) relative paths into a single absolute path.
      It understands . (current directory), .. (parent directory), and combines paths logically.
